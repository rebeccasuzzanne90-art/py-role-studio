    items:
      - question: "What's the difference between payroll compliance and payroll governance?"
        answer: |
          Compliance is meeting the legal minimum; paying correctly, lodging on time, applying the right rules. Governance is the structure that makes compliance sustainable: who owns decisions, how risks are escalated, what controls exist, and whether leadership has the visibility to act. Compliance without governance is fragile.
      - question: "How do I know if I have a payroll compliance risk?"
        answer: |
          In many organisations, payroll risk builds quietly before it becomes visible. Common signs include recurring payroll issues, unclear ownership, heavy reliance on key individuals, poor handoffs from departments upstream, repeated manual workarounds, weak reporting, or leadership discomfort about whether payroll is truly under control.
          
          You do not need a known underpayment to have payroll risk. In many cases, the risk sits in the conditions around payroll long before a failure is confirmed.
      - question: "Do you provide legal advice or industrial interpretation?"
        answer: |
          No. We do not provide legal advice or formal industrial interpretation.
          
          Where legal interpretation is needed, we work alongside the relevant legal and specialist partners and help organisations translate those decisions into practical governance, ownership, and operating actions.
      - question: "Is this just for payroll teams?"
        answer: |
          No. Payroll risk is rarely created by payroll alone.
          
          Many payroll issues are shaped by decisions made across HR, Finance, Operations, rostering, workforce management, systems, and leadership. That is why our work often involves multiple functions, not just the payroll team itself.
      - question: "How is this different from a payroll software implementation partner?"
        answer: |
          Software can be part of the solution, but it does not resolve weak governance, poor decision-making, or unclear accountability on its own.
          
          Our role is not configuration or vendor delivery. Our role is to help organisations put the right structure, guardrails, and governance around payroll change so the underlying risk is not simply carried into a new system.
      - question: "Can you help during a payroll system implementation or transformation?"
        answer: |
          Yes. We can support organisations through payroll-related change by focusing on readiness, governance, decision-making, accountability, and risk oversight.
          
          This may include helping clarify operating guardrails, strengthening stakeholder alignment, identifying dependencies, and supporting safer execution during high-risk change.
      - question: "Can you help train our leaders or other teams on payroll risk?"
        answer: |
          Yes. In many organisations, payroll risk is created upstream by teams who do not fully understand how their decisions affect compliance outcomes.
          
          We can provide targeted education, workshops, and capability uplift for payroll teams, leaders, and payroll-adjacent functions such as HR, Finance, and Operations.
      - question: "Can AI or automation solve payroll compliance issues?"
        answer: |
          AI and automation can improve efficiency, but they do not fix weak governance, poor process design, or unclear decision logic.
          
          In payroll, automation can reduce manual effort, but if the underlying environment is unstable, it can also scale mistakes faster. That is why readiness, guardrails, and control design matter.
      - question: "Why does remediation readiness matter?"
        answer: |
          Many remediation programs become harder, slower, and more expensive because the business starts before the right conditions are in place.
          
          Where governance is weak, ownership is unclear, or dependencies are not properly understood, remediation can create a second layer of risk on top of the original issue.
